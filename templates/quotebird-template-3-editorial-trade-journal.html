The Cover Story

The quoting tool the trades have been waiting a decade for.

Filed by The Quotebird Team · Sacramento, CA · 2026

Quoting hasn't kept up with the trades. A 30-year HVAC business runs on a spreadsheet, a thrice-folded supplier price sheet, and a pile of emailed PDFs. Quotebird is the first quoting tool built around how the work actually happens — at the kitchen table, on a phone, with a customer looking over your shoulder.

You upload your supplier price sheets once. Claude Vision extracts every SKU. You approve the catalog. From that moment on, every quote you send pulls real prices from your catalog — never a fabricated number, never a guess that you have to clean up afterward.

Then you describe the job in plain English and a branded PDF lands in the customer's inbox before they finish their coffee. The accepted ones convert to invoices with a tap. Unforeseen changes are tracked transparently. You mark paid. The dashboard shows you what's owed.

Feature · No. 01

Your supplier sheet, read by the machine.

A meditation on catalog ingestion

The first job a contractor does with Quotebird isn't a quote. It's a coffee-and-photos session. You sit down with your three or four most-used supplier price sheets — Carrier, Rheem, Eaton, the local plumbing wholesaler — and you upload each one.

Claude Sonnet 4, with its vision capability, reads every page. It identifies SKU codes, descriptions, units, and prices. It hands you back a structured list, organized by category, with anything it couldn't read clearly flagged in amber for your eyes.

You review. Twenty minutes, maybe forty if the photos were rough. You approve the rows that look right. You correct or skip the ones it flagged. You hit save.

From that moment, Quotebird knows your prices. Not estimates of your prices — your prices. The next time you ask for a quote that involves a 3-Ton 16 SEER condenser, the line item says $2,650, because that's what you sell it for. Not because the AI guessed.

Inside the Issue · The Product

The screen you'll live in.

A walkthrough of Quotebird's Prompt-to-Quote screen — the one place where the entire product converges. Customer fields up top. A big honest textarea. Three example chips. One generate button. That's the whole interface.
A
Customer fields

Optional but encouraged. Name, email, phone, address. The quote can be drafted without them and emailed later — useful for shop estimates done over the phone.

B
The textarea

Plain English. Equipment, materials, labor estimate, anything special. The AI handles structure, math, and markup. You provide the trade knowledge.

C
Example chips

Tap to pre-fill. Three running prompts — one per common job. Useful when you're at the customer's house and the wording slips your mind.

D
Generate

Quote lands in under thirty seconds. Review screen opens. Every line still editable. Send when you're ready.
