A working day, redrawn

Built for the truck, the cab, and the kitchen table — not the desk.

Most quoting software was designed for people who sit at a computer all day. You're not those people. Quotebird is shaped around how trade work actually happens: in the moment, on a phone, with a customer looking over your shoulder.

i
Onboard once.

Upload your three or four supplier price sheets. Claude Vision extracts the SKUs. You review the catalog over a coffee — done in twenty minutes.

ii
Describe the job.

At the customer's house. "Replace 3-ton condenser, 16 SEER, attic access tight, will reuse line set if leak test passes." That's all.

iii
Send the PDF.

Quote shows up branded with your logo, warranty wording, payment terms. Email goes out before the customer's coffee is cold.

iv
Convert & collect.

"I accept." One tap turns the quote into an invoice. Edit any unforeseen changes — those show up transparently for the customer.

A spreadsheet doesn't shake the customer's hand. Your quote does. Make it look like it came from a shop that gives a damn.
