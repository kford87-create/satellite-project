What it does

Three tools that finally know about each other.

Quotebird isn't a spreadsheet bolted onto a payment processor. It's a catalog, a quoting AI, and an invoicing layer — wrapped in something that opens fast on a five-year-old phone and respects the trade.

01

Your catalog, ingested.

Snap a photo or upload a PDF of your supplier price sheet. Claude Vision extracts every SKU code, description, unit, and price. You review the rows, approve, and it's yours forever.

PhotoPDFReview gate~$0.50 / sheet
02

Quoting that uses your prices.

Describe the job in plain English. The AI retrieves matching SKUs from your catalog by semantic similarity and composes the quote using the exact prices you set. Unmatched items get flagged for you to fill — no fabricated numbers.

Claude Sonnet 4Voyage-3-largepgvector
03

Branded delivery, honest invoicing.

Your logo, warranty wording, payment terms. Customer replies "I accept." One tap converts the quote to an invoice — line edits are tracked as a transparent "Changes from original quote" section, shown to the customer.

ResendReact-PDFChange deltas
04

Built for the truck.

Mobile-first PWA, installable to your home screen. Works offline, syncs later. Designed for a contractor pulling out a phone at the customer's house — not a desk worker with two monitors.

The flow

Onboard once. Then quote forever.

Twenty minutes of catalog setup buys you years of zero-friction quoting. Every job after the first one is a thirty-second conversation between you, the textarea, and a PDF.

01 · UPLOAD

Supplier sheets in

Three or four PDFs. Twenty minutes of your time. Claude Vision reads every SKU; you review and approve.

02 · TYPE

Describe the job

At the kitchen table. Plain English. Customer fields optional. The AI handles structure and math.

03 · SEND

Branded PDF

Logo, warranty, payment terms. Email goes out via Resend. Signed PDF link expires in 90 days.

04 · COLLECT

Accept → invoice → paid

One tap converts. Outstanding widget tracks the rest. Overdue cron flips invoices nightly.

The main screen

Type the job. Send the quote.

This is the screen you'll spend most of your time on. Customer fields up top. A big honest textarea. Three example chips. One generate button. That's it.

A

Optional customer info

Name, email, phone, address. The quote can be drafted without them and emailed later — useful for over-the-phone estimates.

B

The textarea

Plain English. Equipment, materials, labor estimate, anything special. The AI handles structure, math, and markup. You bring the trade knowledge.

C

Example chips

Tap to pre-fill. Useful when you're standing at the customer's house and the wording slips your mind.

D

Generate

Quote in under thirty seconds. Lands on the review screen — every line still editable. Send when ready.
